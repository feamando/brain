The sessions drop off in PetsTable is significantly lower because of three reasons:





1. The registration page is skipped entirely for customers who logged in a previous session, and are redirected to the checkout page directly by the site cookies. HelloFresh also has the same behavior but in the case of PetsTable, the customer is completing registration often and also coming back more often to a previously logged in session. 





2. The higher registration completion rate can also be attributed to high intent and cognitive investment of the customer, who before reaching the registration page has completed 9 steps of the quiz (which sometimes needs research - pup weight, dietary preference, allergies, goals) and selected the plan & recipe selection. The % session for HelloFresh, ThePetsTable and GoodChop remain the same to the Address page. 





3. Plus, increasing propensity to make more premium & health conscious choices for their pets over themselves, drives higher intent in completion of the PetsTable account registration, than that of HelloFresh. 





Given that the registration step isn‚Äôt resulting in customer drop offs, the removal of the page is not likely to increase conversions. The registration page is in fact necessary[a][b][c][d] to verify whether a prospect already has an account, which helps in redirecting existing users to the reactivation page (and adjusting applicable discounts), and prevents them from redundantly navigating the entire signup funnel. After enabling email autopopulation on the registration page from WKND cookie, the friction on this page has declined further. Therefore, we advocate to keep the registration page, make minimal functional changes and improve the sign up CTA to increase the customer intent further to complete checkout.
