Problem Statement

Social media has become a primary hub for culinary inspiration, where people turn to influencers to discover new recipes. While Followers struggle to find the necessary ingredients and an easy way to re-cook them, Influencers look for new revenue streams to be more independent from the heavily controlled platforms and find new ways to expand their reach and engage with their audiences authentically and organically via food.





Opportunity:

Hellofresh sees a huge opportunity to serve both: by transforming influencers' inspiration (recipe) into a shoppable, cookable meal kit for the consumers. 

By bridging the gap as the Expert in operation, warehouses and logistics, we provide customers a way to enjoy these recipes easily and the influencer to serve their followers and create a new revenue stream. The social commerce market is growing rapidly and can represent a huge opportunity for us to attract new customers.





Why now:

We see the urgency to act now, because influencers are already trying to solve this problem with links to ingredient orders  via Instacart or Pinterest partners with Instacart to enable 1-click grocery list generation from saved pins. But today, shoppable content on social media within the food category is often limited to individual food items as opposed to a holistic offering where the ingredients for entire recipes and direct delivery can be purchased.

Executive Summary: 

Research participants responded very positively to the Influencer Marketplace concept, viewing it as a compelling way to turn social media food inspiration into achievable home cooking. The concept resonates because it directly addresses two major friction points: difficulty sourcing niche ingredients, due to cost, waste, and effort, and the common habit of saving recipes that are later forgotten in overcrowded digital collections.

Trust in recipes is driven less by influencer status[a][b] and more by practical signals of feasibility, including strong visuals, clear instructions, and access to the original social media content for verification. Participants place significant responsibility on HelloFresh to ensure recipe quality, either through internal testing and approval or, if full vetting is not feasible, through transparent and prominent community ratings and reviews.

The marketplace is perceived primarily as an occasion-based solution rather than a replacement for everyday meal planning. Participants most strongly associate it with dinner and special occasions such as weekends or hosting, with limited interest in breakfast or lunch use cases. To encourage trial and adoption, users strongly prefer a low-commitment purchasing model, favoring one-time purchases over subscription requirements.[c][d]

Discovery expectations are food-first rather than creator-led, with users wanting to browse and search by ingredient or cuisine instead of influencer names. Finally, participants expressed a need for more robust recipe management tools, including custom organization and scheduling features, to ensure saved recipes resurface at the right time and translate into actual cooking behavior.

	Building Creator Trust

	In this marketplace, users may find it challenging to evaluate creators in the same ways they do on social media.

H2 2025 Reflection

In H2 2025, our product strategy was designed to address unrecovered Conversion Rates (CR) and declining Active Order Rates (AOR). For CR, we successfully launched the Reactivation page revamp and are on progress to launch the One Time Purchase experiment. On the AOR side, we drove Free Add-on take rates to over 90% consistently by closing gaps in the selection and persistence experience, and launched Charge at Checkout.

1. Reactivation Page Revamp. We launched a new, mobile-first reactivation page that reduces the page length by one third with improvements on information architecture and clearer call to action. Also, customers can now choose their free add-ons directly on the reactivation page, instead of after reactivating their accounts. These changes are foundational to run more free for life reactivation campaigns including turkeys on Thanksgiving  (+20% CR YoY). However, some critical features were de-prioritized such as the ability to change cadence and reschedule directly on the reactivation page. 

2. One-Time Purchases (OTP). The technical solution for OTP pivoted from the initial MVP hack to the proper solution due to a technical blocker from CRM as well as higher confidence in the new endpoint following HF rollouts. While this solution saves time in the long-term, the launch week is delayed (likely W2‚Äô2026 - TBC) due to additional time for another technical investigation and (unplanned) capacity constraints.

3. Closing Free Add-on Take Rate. By October we finally reached +90% free add-on take rate for both new and active users consistently. Persisting free add-ons (+40% active TR), developing the multi-add-on pop-up on funnel and reactivation (+5% TR), and migrating bundle offers to Wallet (4.2k customers). While it‚Äôs great that we finally closed down the Wallet take rate gap, a significant portion of Q3 engineering capacity was allocated, which was not originally accounted for in the H2 roadmap.

1. Goal





Our goal is to create a single, unified prospects checkout layout with standardized features that will work centrally for all Mealkit brands (HelloFresh, GreenChef, EveryPlate, and ChefsPlate) and RTE brands (Factor and Youfoodz).[a][b][c][d][e]





Checkout is a critical phase in the acquisition funnel where users register or log in, provide their address, select delivery options, and add payment details.

Unified feature is a shared component that can operate across multiple countries, brands, and user journeys. Once implemented, any new functionality built on this Feature is automatically available wherever the feature is deployed, ensuring consistency across the board. Features list are available on the portal: https://www-staging.hellofresh.com/features?f=DeliverySummaryFeature

Modular checkout is the checkout code setup, which consists of  independent, reusable unified Features.

Checkout layout is a structure for how elements are interconnected as part of it.

2. Problem Statement





The HelloFresh Group has expanded to 8 brands across 18 countries, which requires scalable solutions to avoid isolated work efforts. The prospects checkout process to purchase subscription can be identical across all brands and products, which makes it the first user experience that can be standardized and managed centrally.





In January 2025, the activation user journey scope was transferred from TAM Alliance to Growth Alliance to centralize Checkout ownership within one team. Two brands, GoodChop and Pets Table, remain in TAM. Scope now increased to 31 distinct codes to manage (18 in HelloFresh with 6 in New Mealkits and 7 in RTE added on top). To scale further and grow sustainably, a strong foundation is needed. HelloFresh has set an 2025 objective #1 to accelerate product development velocity for Ready-to-eat (RTE) brands by distributing ownership of core customer journeys across the consumer-facing-alliances (CFAs).





The Reduce Effort Needed to Checkout strategy initiated in 2024 involved refactoring the HelloFresh checkout into a modular checkout with unified features. By the end of 2024, the scope expanded to include RTE and WLML, aiming to unify features across all meal brands. More details are in Modular Checkout. The goal for H1-2025 is to complete feature modularization and unify the layouts for all meal brands.
