Summary[a]

This document outlines the approach and implementation strategy for to experiment with item-based pricing for Good Chop. This experiment will create a Shopify storefront to offer products through both one-time purchases and flexible subscriptions.

The primary objective is to create an new marketplace for Good Chop customers to order products in a flexible way vs the legacy site[b]. With the marketplace, we hope to gain insights into how product uptake, order habits, customer lifetime value, the existing experience. We will also evaluate the operational lift and cost-effectiveness of this new channel.

Strategy Review

Which initiatives are difficult or impossible to test with our current tech infrastructure?

Based on the provided documents, several key initiatives for the Good Chop brand are currently difficult or impossible to test due to foundational gaps in the technical infrastructure.

1. Dynamic and Item-Based Pricing

* The Barrier: The current platform relies on a "SKU-based product definition" (fixed prices for box plans). This architecture explicitly blocks the ability to run price experimentation or support item-based pricing (e.g., pricing specific cuts differently rather than using a flat plan rate).

* Impact: This prevents the testing of dynamic pricing per protein, a key initiative intended to allow customers to select plans and cuts based on varying quality levels (e.g., premium vs. standard cuts). It also hinders the transition to a hybrid e-commerce model that requires "planless" box sizes or premium bulk purchasing.

2. E-Commerce and One-Off Purchases

* The Barrier: There are "foundational gaps" between customer needs and the current HelloFresh core technology regarding e-commerce funnels, multiple subscriptions, and one-time purchases.

* Impact: It is currently difficult to test and enable one-off boxes (purchases without a subscription) or guest checkout capabilities, which are identified as critical for reducing barriers to entry. Capabilities for these features are being built (using Factor Form as a testing ground) to eventually allow for these experiments.

3. Frontend Experimentation and Shared Real Estate

* The Barrier: Running experiments directly on the homepage is not supported "out-of-the-box." For example, during a "LIFT Homepage redesign" test, the team could not allocate traffic on the page itself; allocation had to occur before customers reached the homepage, complicating the testing setup.

* Impact: It is difficult for multiple teams (e.g., Growth and Vertical-specific teams) to test or contribute to the same digital "real estate" (like funnel pages) without hitting significant technical dependencies. A "Contribution Model" is currently being developed to resolve this friction.

4. Data and Tracking

* The Barrier: There is "poor tracking quality" on Web properties and "even less" on RTE (Ready-to-Eat) mobile apps.

* Impact: The lack of a mature tracking infrastructure makes it difficult to accurately measure the impact of complex experiments across Good Chop's digital platforms.

5. Regional Pricing

* The Barrier: The current infrastructure does not support regional pricing variability.

* Impact: This limits Good Chop's ability to test price competitiveness in specific key markets like California, Texas, and Florida, which is a strategic priority for 2025.
