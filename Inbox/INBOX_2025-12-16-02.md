1. Purpose

The Pets Table (TPT) faces a critical retention challenge: our Average Order Rate (AOR) lags behind competitors like Farmer‚Äôs Dog and Ollie, largely due to the lack of proactive, mobile-first customer engagement. This proposal outlines a low-cost, high-velocity strategy to deploy a TPT native mobile app. By leveraging a "webview-first" architecture (React Native shell + existing  web flows), we can bypass expensive native development to deliver v0 (Web Parity) and v1 (Push Notifications). The core value prop isn't the app interface itself, but the notification channel it unlocks‚Äîdriving customers back to the service for order management and upsells, projected to generate ~$1.5M in annualized incremental revenue.

2. Current State / Problem

"Out of sight, out of mind."





* Lagging AOR: TPT‚Äôs AOR is trailing key competitors who utilize native apps to maintain persistent screen real estate and habituation.

* Reactive vs. Proactive: Our current web-only experience relies on customers remembering to visit our site or opening emails (20-25% open rate). We lack a direct, proactive trigger to prompt timely order management.

* Friction: Logging into a mobile website is high-friction compared to persistent app login, discouraging frequent "snacking" interactions where add-on upsells occur.

3. Strategic Solution

Build "The Notification Vehicle" Instead of a costly, multi-quarter native app build, we propose a Webview-First Hybrid App.





* Strategy: Use the Client Platform‚Äôs new React Native infrastructure to wrap our existing mobile web experience.

* Philosophy: "The app is a notification delivery vehicle with a dashboard face. Everything else is a webview."

* Cost/Speed: Minimizes engineering effort by reusing 90% of existing web code. Focuses native development strictly on the "Unlock" features: Push Notifications and Biometric Login.

Problem Statement

Social media has become a primary hub for culinary inspiration, where people turn to influencers to discover new recipes. While Followers struggle to find the necessary ingredients and an easy way to re-cook them, Influencers look for new revenue streams to be more independent from the heavily controlled platforms and find new ways to expand their reach and engage with their audiences authentically and organically via food.





Opportunity:

Hellofresh sees a huge opportunity to serve both: by transforming influencers' inspiration (recipe) into a shoppable, cookable meal kit for the consumers. 

By bridging the gap as the Expert in operation, warehouses and logistics, we provide customers a way to enjoy these recipes easily and the influencer to serve their followers and create a new revenue stream. The social commerce market is growing rapidly and can represent a huge opportunity for us to attract new customers.





Why now:

We see the urgency to act now, because influencers are already trying to solve this problem with links to ingredient orders  via Instacart or Pinterest partners with Instacart to enable 1-click grocery list generation from saved pins. But today, shoppable content on social media within the food category is often limited to individual food items as opposed to a holistic offering where the ingredients for entire recipes and direct delivery can be purchased.
