Product Requirements Document (PRD): Cancellation Funnel Unification

1. Overview & Purpose

This PRD outlines the requirements for unifying the cancellation funnels across all HelloFresh Group brands. The primary goal is to leverage the existing HelloFresh cancellation funnel, including its deflection strategies and cancellation survey, and apply it to the remaining RTE, meal kit and new venture brands. This initiative aims to standardize the cancellation experience, improve customer retention for Factor and other brands, and streamline operational and technical maintenance. Depending on the engineering complexity, we consider two possible rollout scenarios:

1. [preferred] A/B test for all brands excl. Hellofresh, with control = BAU and variation = complete unified funnel. Alternatively, the test can target Factor US customers only.

2. Roll out without an A/B test, with the pre-post performance analysis

Decision about the prioritised rollout scenario will be taken by Karolina Zak/Edward Day once the effort estimation is known.

2. Goals & Non-Goals

Goals:

1. Use Hellofresh Cancellation Funnel UX in the remaining brands: Replicate the proven HelloFresh cancellation flow UX, including deflections and feedback collection, onto the other brands (all meal-kit, all RTE, The PetsTable, GoodChop, Factor Form, ?Factor Programs?[a][b][c][d]).

   1. !! This requirement does not mean using Hellofresh funnel code across brands. The initial engineering proposal is to create a new cancellation funnel module and move the Hellofresh flow pieces into this new cross-brand funnel. Once this is setup we can migrate the funnels to use this new app

2. Easy handling of all funnel variations: all the existing cancellation funnel variations (BAU flow, one-step funnel, one-click funnel, French adjusted funnel) need to be handled in a scalable way and allow reusing existing flows in other brands and markets

3. Standardize Cancellation Surveys: Implement the HelloFresh cancellation survey framework for other brands to gather consistent customer feedback on reasons for cancellation.

4. Unify Deflection Strategies: Make all existing deflection mechanisms available for use (not necessarily enabled) in all other brands cancellation funnels, including:

   1. Special deflections offered in a dedicated page sections:

      1. Benefits of keeping the subscription active (available discounts & credits, loyalty program status) ‚Üí do not show if a customer doesn‚Äôt have a discount nor credit nor loyalty program status

Problem Statement

Social media has become a primary hub for culinary inspiration, where people turn to influencers to discover new recipes. While Followers struggle to find the necessary ingredients and an easy way to re-cook them, Influencers look for new revenue streams to be more independent from the heavily controlled platforms and find new ways to expand their reach and engage with their audiences authentically and organically via food.





Opportunity:

Hellofresh sees a huge opportunity to serve both: by transforming influencers' inspiration (recipe) into a shoppable, cookable meal kit for the consumers. 

By bridging the gap as the Expert in operation, warehouses and logistics, we provide customers a way to enjoy these recipes easily and the influencer to serve their followers and create a new revenue stream. The social commerce market is growing rapidly and can represent a huge opportunity for us to attract new customers.





Why now:

We see the urgency to act now, because influencers are already trying to solve this problem with links to ingredient orders  via Instacart or Pinterest partners with Instacart to enable 1-click grocery list generation from saved pins. But today, shoppable content on social media within the food category is often limited to individual food items as opposed to a holistic offering where the ingredients for entire recipes and direct delivery can be purchased.
