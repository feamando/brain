TAM Product: Context-driven Product Management

	Summary: In 2025, thanks to the growing maturity of AI-based tools and products, the tech industry shifted from theoretical possibility to practical necessity of integrating AI into the daily processes of employees; HelloFresh as well has actively engaged into the exploration, deployment and integration of AI-based tools into the workplace across functions. This document aims to offer each product manager within TAM an easy step-by-step guide and inspiration on how your daily workflow can (and should) change through the integration of AI into every step of your daily workflow.

Problem

Delivery, efficiency expectations of leadership across HelloFresh generally and HelloTech in particular is being recalibrated to address the reality of AI-augmented staff capabilities; simply put - more and faster will be expected from every team member; that would stretch everyone if not mitigated by automation of intelligence-based repetitive work through AI integration.





Principles

The principles for the new AI-enabled product manager ways of working are grounded in the core principles of the New Model proposed by Assaf Ronenon 16.09.2025: 

1. Customer First: Every decision must begin with the customer and be anchored in satisfaction, retention, and lifetime value.

2. One Global System: Wherever possible, we will converge on unified tools, processes, and standards, eliminating duplication.

3. Speed and Agility: We will shorten decision cycles and empower leaders to experiment, learn, and scale quickly.

4. Clear Accountability: Each decision will have a single owner with the authority and responsibility to deliver outcomes.

5. MD-Integrated Organization: Managing Directors (MDs) own the P&L for each geography. Central functions must be responsive, enabling local experimentation while ensuring it aligns with globally defined systems.









Solution

The solution to the growing problem of increased expectations is not only the training and learning of effective use of individual tools by product managers; but ensuring all individual tools can be combined into a comprehensive process, ensuring that core components, assets, artefacts, tools, best practices are reused, expanded, sustained and followed, empowering individuals to achieve peak performance within this new age. It‚Äôs important to establish a set of core principles that should be guiding your work: 





1. You are responsible: whatever you do and whatever you create and submit - there is no substitution from your personal responsibility, your content, data, findings, priorities etc. that might be generated by an AI are, at the end of the day, your responsibility. 

2. Conclusion

This document is not aimed to be comprehensive or cover every single aspect of product management and the SDLC process, this is not only impossible, but also would assume a static reality where processes and approaches would not change. Problem Statement

Social media has become a primary hub for culinary inspiration, where people turn to influencers to discover new recipes. Opportunity:

Hellofresh sees a huge opportunity to serve both: by transforming influencers' inspiration (recipe) into a shoppable, cookable meal kit for the consumers. 

By bridging the gap as the Expert in operation, warehouses and logistics, we provide customers a way to enjoy these recipes easily and the influencer to serve their followers and create a new revenue stream. The social commerce market is growing rapidly and can represent a huge opportunity for us to attract new customers.
