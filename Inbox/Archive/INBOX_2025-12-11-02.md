1. Purpose

The Pets Table (TPT) faces a critical retention challenge: our Average Order Rate (AOR) lags behind competitors like Farmer‚Äôs Dog and Ollie, largely due to the lack of proactive, mobile-first customer engagement. This proposal outlines a low-cost, high-velocity strategy to deploy a TPT native mobile app. By leveraging a "webview-first" architecture (React Native shell + existing web flows), we can bypass expensive native development to deliver v0 (Web Parity) and v1 (Push Notifications). The core value prop isn't the app interface itself, but the notification channel it unlocks‚Äîdriving customers back to the service for order management and upsells, projected to generate ~$1.5M in annualized incremental revenue.

2. Current State / Problem

"Out of sight, out of mind."





* Lagging AOR: TPT‚Äôs AOR is trailing key competitors who utilize native apps to maintain persistent screen real estate and habituation.

* Reactive vs. Proactive: Our current web-only experience relies on customers remembering to visit our site or opening emails (20-25% open rate). We lack a direct, proactive trigger to prompt timely order management.

* Friction: Logging into a mobile website is high-friction compared to biometric app login, discouraging frequent "snacking" interactions where add-on upsells occur.

3. Strategic Solution

Build "The Notification Vehicle" Instead of a costly, multi-quarter native app build, we propose a Webview-First Hybrid App.





* Strategy: Use the Client Platform‚Äôs new React Native infrastructure to wrap our existing mobile web experience.

* Philosophy: "The app is a notification delivery vehicle with a dashboard face. Everything else is a webview."

* Cost/Speed: Minimizes engineering effort by reusing 90% of existing web code. Focuses native development strictly on the "Unlock" features: Push Notifications and Biometric Login.

As generative AI automates many traditional product management tasks‚Äîsuch as writing first-draft user stories, summarizing research, and creating project plans‚Äîthe product manager's primary responsibility shifts from creating detailed, static documentation to curating and maintaining a rich, dynamic, and machine-readable "Project Context Document." This approach fundamentally champions a "build-first" culture. It moves away from lengthy, theoretical debates over specifications and prioritizes rapid, interactive prototyping. The ultimate goal is to accelerate decision-making, significantly increase development velocity, and ensure that all stakeholders‚Äîproduct, design, engineering, and business‚Äîare perpetually aligned around a shared, continuously updated understanding of the customer problem and the proposed solution.

2. Introduction

Context-driven Product Management is a modern approach designed for a technology landscape where generative AI is an active, collaborative participant in the product development lifecycle. It represents a necessary evolution away from the traditional, documentation-heavy product management methodologies prevalent in many large tech companies. The classic PRD, while well-intentioned, frequently became a bottleneck, a static artifact in a dynamic environment.

In the past, product managers spent a significant amount of time creating detailed Product Requirements Documents (PRDs) and specifications to minimize ambiguity before development began. Context-driven Product Management leverages this fundamental economic shift. Instead of asking "What should we build?" based on lengthy documents, it asks, "What should we not choose to build?" by rapidly and inexpensively testing tangible, interactive prototypes with users. Also, customers can now choose their free add-ons directly on the reactivation page, instead of after reactivating their accounts. These changes are foundational to run more free for life reactivation campaigns including turkeys on Thanksgiving  (+20% CR YoY). However, some critical features were de-prioritized such as the ability to change cadence and reschedule directly on the reactivation page.
