Problem Statement

Social media has become a primary hub for culinary inspiration, where people turn to influencers to discover new recipes. While Followers struggle to find the necessary ingredients and an easy way to re-cook them, Influencers look for new revenue streams to be more independent from the heavily controlled platforms and find new ways to expand their reach and engage with their audiences authentically and organically via food.





Opportunity:

Hellofresh sees a huge opportunity to serve both: by transforming influencers' inspiration (recipe) into a shoppable, cookable meal kit for the consumers. 

By bridging the gap as the Expert in operation, warehouses and logistics, we provide customers a way to enjoy these recipes easily and the influencer to serve their followers and create a new revenue stream. The social commerce market is growing rapidly and can represent a huge opportunity for us to attract new customers.





Why now:

We see the urgency to act now, because influencers are already trying to solve this problem with links to ingredient orders  via Instacart or Pinterest partners with Instacart to enable 1-click grocery list generation from saved pins. Executive Summary: 

Research participants responded very positively to the Influencer Marketplace concept, viewing it as a compelling way to turn social media food inspiration into achievable home cooking. The concept resonates because it directly addresses two major friction points: difficulty sourcing niche ingredients, due to cost, waste, and effort, and the common habit of saving recipes that are later forgotten in overcrowded digital collections.

Trust in recipes is driven less by influencer status[a] and more by practical signals of feasibility, including strong visuals, clear instructions, and access to the original social media content for verification. Participants place significant responsibility on HelloFresh to ensure recipe quality, either through internal testing and approval or, if full vetting is not feasible, through transparent and prominent community ratings and reviews.

The marketplace is perceived primarily as an occasion-based solution rather than a replacement for everyday meal planning. Participants most strongly associate it with dinner and special occasions such as weekends or hosting, with limited interest in breakfast or lunch use cases. To encourage trial and adoption, users strongly prefer a low-commitment purchasing model, favoring one-time purchases over subscription requirements.[b]

Discovery expectations are food-first rather than creator-led, with users wanting to browse and search by ingredient or cuisine instead of influencer names. Finally, participants expressed a need for more robust recipe management tools, including custom organization and scheduling features, to ensure saved recipes resurface at the right time and translate into actual cooking behavior.

	Building Creator Trust

	In this marketplace, users may find it challenging to evaluate creators in the same ways they do on social media. 

	* Direct Links to Original Videos: To foster greater trust in creators, half of participants (4 out of 8) suggested the inclusion of direct links on recipe pages. These links should lead back to the influencer's original social media video, providing direct visual instruction and making it simpler for users to verify and find their content.

	Recipe Vetting

	Participants generally expressed skepticism about recipe reliability from unverified creators, with the majority expecting HelloFresh to act as a "safety net" by vetting recipes for safety and feasibility. Without HelloFresh vetting all recipes, users need to be able to have the information to evaluate the recipes themselves.

	* Verified Badge: If HelloFresh chooses to quality test any of the recipes, a visual distinction like a tag or a badge should indicate which recipes have been tested by HelloFresh chefs, in contrast to unverified user-uploaded content.

* Unfiltered Reviews: Users desire a transparent review system that includes recipe ratings, customer feedback, and photos. This system should allow users to view any negative feedback or potential issues, helping them determine the trustworthiness of a recipe creator. 

* Recipe Comments or Q&A: Participants indicated a need for a comment or Q&A section, separate from a typical review system.
