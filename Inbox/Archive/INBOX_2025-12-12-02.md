1. Purpose

The Pets Table (TPT) faces a critical retention challenge: our Average Order Rate (AOR) lags behind competitors like Farmer‚Äôs Dog and Ollie, largely due to the lack of proactive, mobile-first customer engagement. This proposal outlines a low-cost, high-velocity strategy to deploy a TPT native mobile app. By leveraging a "webview-first" architecture (React Native shell + existing  web flows), we can bypass expensive native development to deliver v0 (Web Parity) and v1 (Push Notifications). The core value prop isn't the app interface itself, but the notification channel it unlocks‚Äîdriving customers back to the service for order management and upsells, projected to generate ~$1.5M in annualized incremental revenue.

2. Current State / Problem

"Out of sight, out of mind."





* Lagging AOR: TPT‚Äôs AOR is trailing key competitors who utilize native apps to maintain persistent screen real estate and habituation.

* Reactive vs. Proactive: Our current web-only experience relies on customers remembering to visit our site or opening emails (20-25% open rate). We lack a direct, proactive trigger to prompt timely order management.

* Friction: Logging into a mobile website is high-friction compared to biometric app login, discouraging frequent "snacking" interactions where add-on upsells occur.

3. Strategic Solution

Build "The Notification Vehicle" Instead of a costly, multi-quarter native app build, we propose a Webview-First Hybrid App.





* Strategy: Use the Client Platform‚Äôs new React Native infrastructure to wrap our existing mobile web experience.

* Philosophy: "The app is a notification delivery vehicle with a dashboard face. Everything else is a webview."

* Cost/Speed: Minimizes engineering effort by reusing 90% of existing web code. Focuses native development strictly on the "Unlock" features: Push Notifications and Biometric Login.

5. Technical Strategy

We will leverage the new HelloFresh Client Platform capabilities to avoid building from scratch.





* Shell: React Native "Container" provided by Client Platform.

* Bridge: Two-way communication between Native Shell and Webview (allowing the web page to trigger native push permission prompts).

* Notifications: Integrated with ABBA (HelloFresh's notification infrastructure) and Braze/CRM for campaign management.

ÔªøProduct Requirements Document: Different Portion Percentages

Problem Statement

Dog owners are predominantly mixed feeders, with over 80% combining various food types. This is done to achieve a balanced diet, control costs, and provide variety. The existing TPT product offering, however, primarily supports a 100% caloric intake plan. The "Topper Plan" is our only attempt to address this, but it is limited and often confusing. This represents a significant missed opportunity for TPT to align with a prevalent feeding practice and attract users who may have a lower willingness to spend on a full-calorie plan.

Goal

To introduce different portion percentages in the funnel to align with mixed feeding habits. This will be launched as an experiment to measure the impact on conversion and revenue.

* Key Actions:

   * Remove the "Topper Plan" from the plan selection flow.

   * Introduce 50% and 75% portion options alongside the 100% plan for fresh and airdried plan on plans selection (funnel), change plan (active experience) and change plan after updating pet profile

   * Expose the option to choose different percentages in the funnel only to users who indicate they are mixed feeders by adding a new question to the quiz. Active users should be able to see all options.

   * Apply these portion options to single-product plans (Fresh, Air-Dried, and eventually Pantry Fresh). The Mixed plan will remain a 100% portion plan only.

Experimentation Plan

We will run an A/B test to validate the impact of these changes. We can consider the following options:

* Option 1: Full Funnel A/B Test: We allocate in the beginning of the quiz. Control group goes through the BAU funnel, the Variation group sees the new question. Based on the submitted answer in the new question, the user might be exposed to the feature of different portion percentages on the plan cards. Main metric - Gross CVR from the 1st question in the funnel.

* Option 2: Full Funnel A/B/C Test: We allocate in the beginning of the quiz. Control group goes through the BAU funnel, the B Variation group sees the new question, but does not see the mixing. Variation C: Based on the submitted answer in the new question, the user might be exposed to the feature of different portion percentages on the plan cards.

Main metric - Gross CVR from the 1st question in the funnel.

* Option 3: Pre-post on the question and A/B Test on the plans page: All users are subject to the question (which will be tested as an pre-post analysis), but only Variation users will be able to be exposed to the portion % feature.

* Option 4: Pre-post on the question and A/B/C Test on the plans page: All users are subject to the question (which will be tested as an pre-post analysis), but only B and C Variation users will be able to be exposed to the portion % feature. On top, Variation C will be defaulted to a 50% plan when landing on the plans page
