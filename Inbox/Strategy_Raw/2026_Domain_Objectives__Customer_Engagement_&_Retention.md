 Customer Engagement & Retention Domain 2026

For 2026, our strategy is built on three parallel workstreams. Our foundational priority is to perfect a meal selection journey for our upcoming 500-meal menu that feels intuitive and personally curated for every customer. Alongside this, we will transform our growth model by attracting a massive, engaged user base to our app with compelling, free-to-use content and tools. A modernized loyalty program will unify these efforts, rewarding engagement across our entire ecosystem to fuel a more sustainable, customer-centric growth engine.





1. Objective: Deliver a World-Class Meal Selection Experience for the 500-Meal Menu

Measure of success: Increase Orders Confirmed from [x] to [y], an increase of [TBC]% 

Objective Owner: Jama Musse

Investment Type: Existing    |  HFG Top 5 Strategy Impact:  2. XL Menu Expansion 

Description: Expanding our menu to 500 meals allows us to provide sufficient ‚Äúrelevant choice‚Äù for every customer segment, across both meal kit (MK) and ready-to-eat (RTE) brands. To unlock this potential and avoid choice paralysis, we will use personalization to create a meal selection journey so intelligent and intuitive that each customer feels the menu was curated just for them. This experience will be powered by an enriched customer profile that combines explicit inputs and implicit signals. Specifically, it will unite customers‚Äô taste preferences (captured during onboarding and editable at any time) and recipe feedback (such as ratings and comments) with a full spectrum of behavioral data, from menu browsing history to ordering patterns. This unified profile will allow us to intelligently rank recipes and engage in tailored merchandising that creates a sense of weekly novelty and discovery, so customers can effortlessly find meals they will love. Nailing this journey of seamless discovery is a critical driver of retention and the foundation of our product-led-growth strategy.

Measure of success: Increase daily active users (DAU) for the HelloFresh app from 700k to 5m  

Objective Owner: Evan Weintraub

Investment Type: New    |  HFG Top 5 Strategy Impact:4. App Experience

For those who start ordering, our goal is to make the app a daily habit. We will achieve this by utilizing targeted in-app messaging and merchandising to highlight the most exciting aspects of our weekly menu, and by providing value beyond order management with features such as an interactive, voice-activated cooking companion. Ultimately, this dual focus on broad audience acquisition and deep engagement with our ordering customers will fuel a more sustainable, customer-centric growth engine, thereby increasing retention.

FAQs





1. What were hotly debated investment priorities that did not make the cut?

To ensure ruthless focus, we deliberately limited ourselves to the stated priorities. Elements of deprioritized objectives may be integrated as features supporting our core goals, but they will not be primary, resource-driving objectives for 2026.





Customer Care: Over 15% of our active customers contact us to report errors, post-cutoff changes, or account issues, making ‚Äúunhappy path‚Äù management critical to retention as our offerings grow more complex. In 2025, we will launch a global chatbot, expand self-serve reporting to all brands with a 50% target, and upgrade agent tooling and our $200M compensation platform. With current resourcing, we believe we can automate most asynchronous scenarios by 2026, significantly reducing agent load while raising CSAT and lowering costs. Given priorities 1‚Äì3, Customer Care will remain stable through 2026, with broader automation acceleration considered in 2027.





Variable Pricing: For 2026, we are consciously deferring a full implementation of variable pricing to focus on our most critical priority: solving the foundational challenges of the 500-meal menu. This decision marks a strategic pivot for the pricing domain. After heavily investing in new capabilities like regional pricing and enhanced price testing in 2024 and 2025, our focus in 2026 will shift from building new infrastructure to leveraging the powerful tools we now have. We will continue to validate our hypotheses on the topic of variable pricing by running targeted, low-effort experiments, such as testing new ways to display surcharges.





Subscription flexibility: Improving subscription flexibility is a key part of our long-term vision and a frequent request from our customers. While progress on this topic has been historically slow due to foundational tech constraints, major platform improvements now underway are resolving these core blockers. As a result, initiatives that would have previously taken months of complex, cross-functional work could be delivered as features in substantially less time. For 2026, while subscription flexibility is not a domain objective, we will commit a dedicated team to deliver the most impactful, strategically aligned features (e.g., one-time purchases, non-weekly delivery frequencies, etc.). The size of the investment and the priority of features to build will be determined during Yearly Planning.





Cross-Selling: Not a main priority for 2026. We will learn from the first pilot in Q3 to cross-sell Factor meals for HF-US customers, and then decide on next steps once our plans for 500 meals are fully developed. We also do not plan any changes to the brand setup, such as merging mealkit brands.





RAF (as historically defined): We recognize that our traditional Refer-a-Friend program was a significant area of underperformance in 2025 and that a new approach is required. For 2026, our vision is to evolve RAF from a standalone acquisition tool into a true virality engine, fully integrated into our loyalty program (Objective #3). This shift allows us to reward a broader range of advocacy behaviors beyond a simple referral, making it just one of several ways a customer can earn rewards. This integration is an active and resourced project for 2026, not a deprioritization. The goal is to make all forms of customer advocacy easier and more rewarding, turning our most passionate customers into a more powerful and efficient growth channel.





2. Where are the opportunities for GenAI applications in your domain? GenAI is a critical accelerator for our objectives. It will be key to delivering hyper-personalized discovery and recommendations for the 500-meal menu. For our daily engagement goal, it will power new and innovative content experiences, such as generating recipes from user inputs. While no longer a standalone objective, GenAI remains the core technology for potential future features like search-based discovery, Voice cooking companion or chatbots, which will be evaluated based on their ability to drive our primary objectives.





3. What are you doing to improve the customer experience? We are transforming the customer experience by focusing on three foundational pillars: First, building a best-in-class, seamless, and personalized shopping experience for our expanded menu. Second, evolving our app into a daily-use food companion that provides value to all users, whether they are ordering or not. Third, implementing a modern loyalty program that rewards customers for their engagement with our ecosystem, fostering a deeper, more resilient relationship with our brands.





4. What are the known risks or obstacles to achieving these objectives? Our biggest risk is resource contention, as the talent required to build the XXL menu experience directly competes with the resources needed for new app engagement features. This forces difficult trade-offs between initiatives with short-term, measurable impact and our long-term strategic bet on engagement. Success will require ruthless prioritization and a disciplined, ‚Äúswarming‚Äù approach. At the same time, we are a late-mover in a crowded market of established recipe apps. Winning will require us to ship truly differentiated features, iterate quickly, and execute a strong go-to-market strategy to generate the traffic needed to start our engagement flywheel. Finally, as we innovate, we must carefully manage the impact on our active subscriber base, who make up the majority (ca. 77%) of our daily app users. We face a dual risk of harming their experience through user confusion (e.g., seeing 15k recipes in our archive but only being able to order 500) and sacrificing direct business value by replacing high-performing features, such as the RAF bottom nav entry point, with new engagement tools.

1. Introduction: From Meal Kit Service to Daily Food Companion

As HelloFresh embarks on its 2026 journey to "Refresh Our Promise" and define the future of food, our success hinges on fundamentally transforming our customer relationships. Our ambition is to evolve beyond the weekly box, becoming an indispensable, daily partner in our customers' kitchens. This transformation is not just an ambition; it is an urgent necessity for returning to growth.

Today, our biggest challenge is customer retention. The average North American customer purchases 5.5 boxes over the course of a year (compared to 5.0 boxes for the international markets), but only 50% reach a second order, and a mere 33% get to a fourth order before cancelling (compared to 66% and 41% for international markets). This reality underscores the need to shift from a transactional service to a vibrant, engaging food ecosystem. These objectives are therefore focused on our most critical lever for returning to sustainable, profitable growth: deepening our daily relationship with customers to drive retention and increase lifetime value.

This document outlines the strategic objectives for the Active Customer Experience domain. We plan to drive daily digital engagement in order to deepen customer connections and cultivate unwavering loyalty. This domain directly supports the company-wide strategic pillars of Daily Customer Engagement and Inspiring Brand & Marketing, ensuring every interaction builds satisfaction and turns customers into passionate advocates. The strategic direction herein will guide the Functional Yearly Plans across Customer Care, Global Actives, NA Product, INTL Product, NA Marketing, INTL Marketing, Growth Alliance, Consumer Services Alliance, MarTech Alliance, and the Actives Journey Alliance.
