1. What were the hotly debated deliverables/big rocks that did not make the cut? I.e. what's below the line?

Due to resource swarming for P500 and DAU:

1. We moved the former RTE Expansion squads into DAU and Shopping experience teams. This led us to deprioritize Factor Programs improvements, and put programs on KTLO. We have in the past weeks improved the initial offering, and have created a‚Äù Programs‚Äù to ‚ÄúClassic Factor‚Äù off boarding experience for customers looking to end their program. 

   1. Proposal: Move Programs to the Shopping Guidance squad who we believe can pick up the Active customer experience improvements in Q2 and then build out our bundling/3rd party offering in H2 of 2026.

2. The planned further expansion of cross-selling into additional brands, markets and models (e.g. OTP model of cross-selling) has been scaled down, with no plans for The Pets Table/Good Chop cross-selling of boxes currently planned for 2026.  

   1. Proposal: Move the scope of cross-selling of TPT and Good Chop into the roadmaps of respective team brands, with the objective to evaluate cost/benefit for the new experience within Q3/4 2026. 









2. What did you achieve already in 2025, and what are your remaining deliverables? 

                 The team has successfully delivered against several key objectives for 2025 defined below: 

* Factor: Launched New Integrated Factor Mobile App, Handed over Factor to Core teams, Launched Factor Programs MVP, Launched Trial Box for Factor Form.

* HelloFresh:  Launched Cross Selling MVP Experience.

* Good Chop: Migrated to Wallet Technology, Delivered Cut Details and Search in Funnel, Launched Mobile-Friendly Cards in Funnel/Menu Display, 

* The Pets Table:  Launched New Cadences, Unblocked Menu Expansion through Optimized SKUs, Launched Allergy Capture & Presentation, Enabled Changing Daily Calories.





 Remaining deliverables for 2025 include: 

* Factor Form: Multiple Cadences for Items (week 44), One-Time Purchase (week 48).  

* HelloFresh: Occasion-based Boxes (week 44).

* Good Chop: One Time Purchase in Funnel (week 50), Charge at Checkout (week 46), Reactivations (week 52)

* The Pets Table: Reactivation Experience (week 43), Charge at Checkout (week 48), Price Test for The Pets Table (week 50).

                               

   3. What were your biggest misses and learnings in 2025? Are there any setbacks and lessons to propel growth and success in the future?

Scaling Factor Form, while maintaining the HF subscription-based infrastructure has posed a significant drag in 2025 as our BAU tools, configurations and services are poorly suited to a vertical where customers expect complete flexibility in their purchasing; coupled with struggles in achieving product market fit in some of the spaces this has resulted in underperformance of the sub-brand in 2025. For Good Chop, similarly, the application of certain broad solutions such as Wallet, Free For Life has resulted in significantly higher technical debt costs for the team, hindering the ability to innovate on the product at the velocity needed to compete (new processes and changes to the data models proposed in 2026 address the capacity of the team to drive change within core systems). 





Finally, ongoing issues with processing payments from customers continue to hinder the performance of the brand and have been slow to be addressed due to the inability to identify a clear root cause of performance degradation





   4. What are the known risks or obstacles to achieving your 2026-YP?

Our biggest risk for our New Ventures in 2026 continues to lie in our back end infrastructure and data model. As these continue to be modelled after a weekly Mealkit Subscription, leading to 2 key point of failure for New Ventures: (1) Increased complexity as we add new ‚Äúproducts‚Äù instead of recipes creating unsustainable SKU permutations, limiting the number of products/variations we can sell; (2) Lack of true ecommerce selling features (true OTP features such as tiered and item pricing) leading to a disadvantage of our brands for customers comparing us to the flexible models and offers on the market and forcing us to contort offers to static box sizes.

Our strategy to reach 5M daily active users (DAU) is to transform the HelloFresh app into a daily food companion. We will begin by attracting a new, broad audience with compelling, free-to-use tools. This audience-first strategy kicks off in Q4 2025 with the Social Recipe Bridge MVP, which allows app users to transform inspiring recipes from social media into a personal cookbook. In Q1, we will enhance the Social Recipe Bridge by improving our recipe parsing AI and introducing new import methods. As our user base grows, our Q2 focus will shift to creating a sticky, daily-use experience with the launch of the Discovery Hub, a personalized screen that surfaces a wide range of relevant and inspiring food content. For the second half of the year, we will focus on helping users express their creativity, avoid food waste, and tailor recipes to their household's tastes by introducing new recipe creation and customization tools.

Concurrently, we will deepen engagement with our subscribers by embedding the app into their daily cooking journey. Our first step in Q4 2025 is to introduce a simple Meal Planner that allows users to schedule their meals for the week. This creates timely communication opportunities; for instance, we can remind them on Tuesday to check pantry ingredients for a Wednesday meal, and on Wednesday ask if they are ready to start cooking. In Q1, we will double down on improving the cooking experience itself by launching an AI Cooking Assistant that helps users bring recipes to life. Besides showing step-by-step instructions with videos, it will help users with ingredient swaps, timers, troubleshooting and even splitting instructions between multiple chefs. To drive long-term habit formation, in Q2 we will introduce gamified Cooking Programs to give our users a clear sense of achievement and progression. Our strategy for the second half of the year is to then radically reduce friction around meal planning, culminating in the launch of solutions like a one-click and collaborative meal planner for households.
