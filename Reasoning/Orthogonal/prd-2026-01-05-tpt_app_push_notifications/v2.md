## Executive Summary of Challenges

The v1 PRD presents a reasonable case for push notifications but has several critical gaps that could undermine success:

1. **Unvalidated Core Assumption**: The 5% AOR lift projection lacks TPT-specific evidence
2. **Missing User Research**: No customer validation of notification preferences
3. **Incomplete Cost-Benefit**: Development costs not weighed against $1.5M projection
4. **Platform Strategy Gap**: iOS vs Android decision deferred but critical for ROI
5. **Measurement Framework Weak**: Success metrics lack statistical rigor

## Detailed Challenges

### Challenge 1: Revenue Projection Lacks TPT-Specific Validation
**Severity: CRITICAL**

**Issue:** The $1.5M incremental revenue and 5% AOR lift are stated as facts but traced only to "2026 BP" without TPT-specific evidence.

**Why It Matters:**
- Industry benchmarks for push notification effectiveness vary wildly (2-15% engagement)
- Pet food subscription behavior may differ from meal kit patterns
- No A/B test or pilot data supports these projections

**Questions:**
- What is the current AOR for TPT customers?
- How was the 5% lift calculated?
- What is the confidence interval on $1.5M projection?

**Recommended Change:**
Add sensitivity analysis showing scenarios: pessimistic (2% lift), expected (5% lift), optimistic (8% lift) with corresponding revenue impact.

### Challenge 2: Missing Customer Research
**Severity: CRITICAL**

**Issue:** No customer interviews, surveys, or usability testing validate that TPT customers want push notifications.

**Why It Matters:**
- Pet parents may have different app engagement patterns than meal kit customers
- Notification fatigue is a real risk without understanding preferences

**Questions:**

## Alternative Proposals

### Alternative A: SMS-First with Push as Upgrade
Instead of app-first push notifications, launch SMS delivery notifications immediately (no app required), then migrate engaged users to push as app adoption grows.

**Pros:**
- Faster time-to-value (no app development)
- Higher reach (all customers, not just app users)
- Lower technical complexity

**Cons:**
- Ongoing SMS costs
- Less rich notification experience
- Doesn't drive app adoption

### Alternative B: Phased Rollout with Validation Gates
Before building Phase 2 & 3, require Phase 1 to hit minimum metrics:
- Push opt-in >50%
- Notification open rate >10%
- No increase in support tickets about notification issues

If gates not met, pivot or pause investment.

**Pros:**
- Reduces risk of building features nobody uses
- Creates learning loops
- Allows resource reallocation if needed

**Cons:**
- Slower to full feature set
- May frustrate stakeholders wanting complete solution
